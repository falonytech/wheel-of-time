Let me start by giving the ending first on this one. I do not like the show. Amazon released season one of this series and I was excited to see the books brought to life. I figured that this was an opportunity for a more main stream audience to experience a story I have loved since high school. After the first four episodes, I knew that they were not doing the books justice, but I still held out some hope that the show would not venture too far from the books. By the end of the series, the story had ventured so far from the books that I no longer recognized the characters, and will not continue to watch the show heading into season two.

The changes are legion and range from changes to the mechanics and lore of the world, to deep changes to the characters and their motivations, to changes in the over all themes of the story, done for political reasons. The show and book series are now brought into the ongoing culture war between progressivism and conservatism. I will try to mostly stay away from some of the more ‘culture war’ aspects of the changes, but there are going to be some points where I cannot help but point some of it out. First, let me say that while some of the characters have been race swapped, this will not be an area I focus time on. While there are some places where the more diverse casting makes little sense, and I will point that out, overall I am less bothered by it and if a good actor is bringing the character to life, matching the looks describes in the book is a little less important.

First, I plan on going through notes of each episode. I will attempt to point out changes as I see them, especially if they were not necessary. It will not be an exhaustive list, though I did just re-read book one of the series, I can’t remember every little detail. I will then summarize some of the critical changes to lore, mechanics, and characters. This will be kind of long, so forgive me, and I am not a professional writer, so don’t expect this to be written like I am one. What I think I will show is that when almost every scene is different than from the book, and is either wholy made up or carries such a different context and meaning, that it cannot be considered the same scene, it is difficult to call this an adaptation. When the world's lore, mechanics, and overall feel is so drastically different, it is difficult to consider this respectful of the source material and one must ask why were the changes made? I think it is more accurate to say that this is Rafe Judkin's Wheel of Time loosely based on the world that Robert Jordan built. This is the story Rafe Judkins wants to tell, not the one Robert Jordan told.
